## COMPLETE WORKFLOW

### PHASE 1: PRE-PRODUCTION (Weeks 1-4)

#### 1.1 Voice Casting

**Narrator Role**
- **Character:** The Narrator (omniscient storyteller)
- **Requirements:**
  - Professional voice actor with theatrical training
  - Ability to convey warmth, wisdom, and emotional range
  - Clear articulation for narration
  - Experience with literary narration
- **Audition Material:** Prologue and epilogue sections
- **Recording: 8-10 sessions** for approximately 45 minutes of narration

**Philip Freeman (Protagonist)**
- **Character:** Young visionary programmer, 25-30 years old
- **Voice Requirements:**
  - Warm, intelligent tone with underlying passion
  - Range from contemplative to determined to vulnerable
  - Ability to convey technical precision and emotional depth
  - Chemistry with Sally's voice
- **Sample Dialogue:** Opening scene + wedding vows
- **Recording: 12-15 sessions** for approximately 30 minutes of dialogue

**Sally Stone (Protagonist)**
- **Character:** Artist/musician, late 20s
- **Voice Requirements:**
  - Clear, expressive, musical quality
  - Natural performer with perfect diction
  - Range from curious to confident to deeply emotional
  - Complementary tone with Philip's voice
- **Sample Dialogue:** Coffee shop scene + wedding vows
- **Recording: 12-15 sessions** for approximately 25 minutes of dialogue

**Supporting/Cameo Characters**
- Additional cast as needed for scenes
- Estimated 5-10 additional voice actors
- 5-10 sessions each for brief appearances

#### 1.2 Music Composition & Licensing

**Original Score (Recommended)**
- **Composer Role:** Create original instrumental compositions for:
  - Opening theme (3-5 minutes)
  - Scene underscore (15-20 minutes total)
  - Wedding ceremony music (5-7 minutes)
  - Closing theme (2-3 minutes)
- **Total Music Duration:** 25-35 minutes
- **Style:** Contemporary orchestral with emotional resonance
- **Budget:** $3,000-$8,000 for professional composer

**Alternative: Licensed Music**
- Source pre-existing compositions via Shutterstock Music, Epidemic Sound, etc.
- Cost: $500-$2,000 for all-inclusive license
- Consider: May lack custom quality and thematic unity

#### 1.3 Sound Design Planning

**Ambient Sounds Needed:**
- City sounds (traffic, distant sirens, ambient noise)
- Loft ambience (subtle hum of electronics)
- Coffee shop bustle
- Ceremonial space acoustics

**Sound Effects Needed:**
- Keyboard typing (various styles)
- UI interactions if shown
- Transition effects
- Environmental sounds (wind, rain, etc.)

**Sources:**
- Freesound.org (free, creative commons)
- Epidemic Sound (subscription)
- Shutterstock Sound (per-track licensing)
- Professional sound designer (custom creation)

### PHASE 2: RECORDING & PRODUCTION (Weeks 4-12)

#### 2.1 Voice Recording Schedule

**Studio Requirements:**
- Professional recording studio with soundproofing
- High-quality microphone (Neumann U87 or equivalent)
- Audio interface (RME Fireface UFX2 or equivalent)
- Monitoring headphones
- Experienced sound engineer
- Director for voice performance guidance

**Recording Sessions:**
- **Narrator Sessions:** 8-10 sessions × 2 hours = 16-20 hours
- **Philip Sessions:** 12-15 sessions × 2 hours = 24-30 hours
- **Sally Sessions:** 12-15 sessions × 2 hours = 24-30 hours
- **Supporting Cast:** 5-10 hours
- **Subtotal:** 69-95 hours of studio time
- **Studio Cost (at $150/hour):** $10,350-$14,250

#### 2.2 Voice Recording Breakdown by Scene

**Prologue:** 25 minutes narration
- 1-2 sessions with narrator
- Record in sections for flexibility

**Act 1, Scene 1 (Loft):**
- 5 minutes narration
- 3 minutes dialogue (Philip)
- 1 session each (narrator, Philip)

**Act 1, Scene 2 (Coffee Shop):**
- 3 minutes narration
- 7 minutes dialogue (Philip & Sally)
- 2 sessions (1 narrator, 1 for both actors together)

**Act 1, Scene 3 (Gathering):**
- 4 minutes narration
- 1 session narrator

**Act 2, Scene 1 (Groom's Room):**
- 4 minutes narration
- 1 minute dialogue (Philip internal monologue)
- 1-2 sessions

**Act 2, Scene 2 (Bride's Room):**
- 3 minutes narration
- 2 minutes dialogue (Sally internal)
- 1-2 sessions

**Act 2, Scene 3 (Ceremony Entrance):**
- 2 minutes narration
- 1 minute dialogue (exchange)
- 1 session

**Act 2, Scene 4 (Vows):**
- 2 minutes narration
- 8 minutes dialogue (complete vows)
- 1-2 sessions (very important, needs multiple takes)

**Act 2, Scene 5 (Kiss & Celebration):**
- 3 minutes narration
- 1 session

**Act 3, Scene 1 (Studio, Five Years Later):**
- 2 minutes narration
- 2 minutes dialogue
- 1 session

**Act 3, Scene 2 (Rooftop):**
- 2 minutes narration
- 3 minutes dialogue
- 1 session

**Epilogue:**
- 8 minutes narration
- 1-2 sessions with narrator

#### 2.3 Recording Tips for Voice Actors

**Preparation:**
- Provide actors with SPKML screenplay in advance
- Hold character briefing session before recording
- Discuss emotional arc and character voice preferences
- Create character voice reference (samples of desired tone)

**Recording Quality Standards:**
- Consistent microphone placement
- Minimal background noise
- Consistent levels throughout
- Multiple takes of each line (minimum 2-3)
- Record "room tone" for editing

**Post-Recording:**
- Normalize audio levels
- Remove clicks, pops, mouth sounds
- Add light compression for consistency
- Fade in/out for smooth editing
- Label all files clearly

### PHASE 3: AUDIO PRODUCTION (Weeks 12-16)

#### 3.1 Audio Mixing & Mastering

**Mixing Process:**
1. **Dialogue Pass:** Ensure all voice lines are clean and at proper levels
   - Philip dialogue: consistent level
   - Sally dialogue: complementary to Philip
   - Narrator dialogue: slightly elevated, authoritative

2. **Narration Pass:** Process all narration
   - Smooth levels across all passages
   - Add subtle EQ for clarity
   - Apply light reverb for cinematic quality

3. **Music Pass:** Integrate original score/licensed music
   - Underscore for scenes (subtle, ~-20dB below dialogue)
   - Theme music (fuller, ~-15dB below dialogue)
   - Transition music (allows breathing room)

4. **Sound Effects Pass:** Layer ambient and effects
   - Ambient beds (~-25 to -30dB)
   - Effects (varying levels as appropriate)
   - Ensure effects don't muddy dialogue

5. **Final Mix:** Blend all elements
   - Dialogue as primary focus
   - Music supporting mood
   - Effects and ambience creating atmosphere
   - Consistent loudness (-18dB LUFS for streaming)

**Mixing Software:**
- Professional: Pro Tools, Logic Pro, Nuendo
- Open Source: Audacity, Reaper
- Cost: $300-$600 for perpetual licenses

#### 3.2 Audio Specifications for Different Formats

**Streaming/Web Version:**
- Format: MP3, AAC, or Opus
- Bitrate: 192 kbps (excellent quality, manageable file size)
- Sample Rate: 44.1 kHz
- Channels: Stereo
- Estimated file size: ~45 MB per hour

**High-Quality Version:**
- Format: FLAC (lossless)
- Bitrate: 1411 kbps (lossless)
- Sample Rate: 48 kHz
- Channels: Stereo
- Estimated file size: ~250 MB per hour

**eBook/EPUB Version:**
- Format: AAC or MP3
- Bitrate: 128-192 kbps
- Sample Rate: 44.1 kHz
- Channels: Stereo
- Estimated file size: ~20-30 MB per hour

### PHASE 4: SCREENPLAY INTEGRATION (Weeks 16-20)

#### 4.1 Audio File Naming Convention

```
[SCENE-TYPE]_[ACT]_[SCENE]_[CHARACTER]_[TAKE].mp3

Examples:
- narration_prologue_001.mp3
- dialogue_act1_scene1_philip_001.mp3
- dialogue_act2_scene4_vows_sally_001.mp3
- ambient_act1_scene1_loft_001.mp3
- music_theme_opening.mp3
```

#### 4.2 SPKML Audio Reference Updates

Update the screenplay SPKML with audio file references:

```xml
<narration type="exposition" 
           voice-id="narrator-voice"
           audio-src="audio/narration/prologue.mp3"
           duration="25">
  [Narration text...]
</narration>

<dialogue character="Philip" 
          voice-id="philip-voice"
          audio-src="audio/dialogue/act1_scene1_philip_001.mp3"
          duration="4">
  [Dialogue text...]
</dialogue>

<sound type="ambient"
       src="audio/ambient/loft_ambience.mp3"
       duration="300"
       volume="25">
  <description>City sounds through window</description>
</sound>

<sound type="music"
       src="audio/music/theme_opening.mp3"
       duration="180"
       volume="40">
  <description>Original score opening theme</description>
</sound>
```

#### 4.3 Audio Synchronization

**Creating Sync Markers:**
1. Record precise timing for each line
2. Create JSON sync manifest:

```json
{
  "version": "1.0.0",
  "screenplay": "CodeBase_Love_Story_Wedding_Celebration",
  "scenes": [
    {
      "scene_id": "prologue",
      "type": "narration",
      "audio_file": "narration/prologue.mp3",
      "duration": 25,
      "cue_points": [
        {"time": 0, "text_start": "Every great story arrives..."},
        {"time": 5, "text_start": "A moment when two hearts..."},
        {"time": 12, "text_start": "Today, they marry."}
      ]
    },
    {
      "scene_id": "act1_scene1",
      "type": "scene",
      "location": "INT. LOFT - MORNING",
      "elements": [
        {
          "type": "narration",
          "audio_file": "narration/act1_scene1_opening.mp3",
          "duration": 12
        },
        {
          "type": "dialogue",
          "character": "Philip",
          "audio_file": "dialogue/act1_scene1_philip_001.mp3",
          "duration": 4,
          "line": "Don't mess this up..."
        }
      ]
    }
  ]
}
```

### PHASE 5: RENDERING & PUBLISHING (Weeks 20-24)

#### 5.1 Interactive HTML Version

**Features:**
- Scene-by-scene navigation
- Embedded audio player with:
  - Play/pause controls
  - Progress bar with sync to text
  - Volume control
  - Playback speed adjustment (0.75x, 1x, 1.25x, 1.5x)
- Character profiles modal
- Responsive design (mobile, tablet, desktop)
- Accessibility (captions, transcripts)

**Technology Stack:**
- HTML5 for structure
- CSS3 for styling
- JavaScript for interactivity
- Audio.js or similar for HTML5 audio player

**Build Process:**
```bash
# Transform SPKML to HTML structure
xsltproc screenplay-to-html.xslt \
  CodeBase_Love_Story_Wedding_Celebration.spkml > base.html

# Add audio player JavaScript
# Integrate SPKML audio manifest
# Add responsive styling
# Create character profile pages

# Test across browsers:
# - Chrome/Chromium
# - Firefox
# - Safari
# - Mobile browsers
```

**Expected File Size:**
- HTML: 500 KB
- CSS: 100 KB
- JavaScript: 200 KB
- Total: ~800 KB (plus audio files served separately)

#### 5.2 PDF Script Version

**Features:**
- Professional screenplay formatting (standard Courier 12pt)
- Character names in CAPS
- Parentheticals for vocal direction
- Scene headings (INT./EXT., location, time)
- Page numbers
- Professional two-column layout (optional)

**Generation Process:**
```bash
# Transform SPKML to PDF-ready HTML
xsltproc screenplay-to-pdf.xslt \
  CodeBase_Love_Story_Wedding_Celebration.spkml > screenplay.html

# Convert HTML to PDF
wkhtmltopdf --print-media-type \
  --dpi 300 \
  --page-size A4 \
  screenplay.html CodeBase_Screenplay.pdf

# Or using Prince:
prince screenplay.html -o CodeBase_Screenplay.pdf
```

**PDF Specifications:**
- Format: A4 or Letter (8.5" × 11")
- Font: Courier New, 12pt
- Margins: 1" (top/bottom), 1.25" (left/right)
- Spacing: Single-spaced dialogue, double-spaced scene headings
- File Size: 2-5 MB
- Pages: Approximately 35-45 pages

#### 5.3 EPUB eBook Version

**Features:**
- Reflowable text (adapts to device screen size)
- Embedded fonts
- Navigation document (NCX)
- Metadata (author, title, cover image)
- Audio references (links to external audio files)
- Full transcript of dialogue and narration

**Structure:**
```
codebook-ebook.epub/
├── OEBPS/
│   ├── content.opf (manifest and package document)
│   ├── toc.ncx (table of contents)
│   ├── styles.css
│   ├── cover.xhtml
│   ├── titlepage.xhtml
│   ├── prologue.xhtml
│   ├── act1.xhtml
│   ├── act2.xhtml
│   ├── act3.xhtml
│   ├── epilogue.xhtml
│   └── images/
│       └── cover.jpg
├── META-INF/
│   └── container.xml
└── mimetype
```

**Generation Process:**
```bash
# Create EPUB structure from SPKML
xsltproc screenplay-to-epub.xslt \
  CodeBase_Love_Story_Wedding_Celebration.spkml

# Package into EPUB
zip -r codebook-ebook.epub \
  OEBPS/ META-INF/ mimetype
```

**EPUB Specifications:**
- EPUB Version: 3.0
- File Size: 2-3 MB
- Compatible Readers:
  - Apple Books
  - Amazon Kindle
  - Google Play Books
  - Kobo
  - Any EPUB-compatible reader

#### 5.4 Audiobook Version

**Features:**
- Complete narrated experience
- Professional voice actors
- Original music and sound design
- No on-screen text (audio-first)
- Chaptered for easy navigation
- Multiple audio format options

**Audio Specifications:**

**Standard Audiobook (M4B, for Apple Books/Audible):**
- Format: M4B (MPEG-4 Audio Book)
- Codec: AAC
- Bitrate: 128-192 kbps
- Sample Rate: 44.1 kHz
- Chapters: 12-15 chapters (one per scene plus intro/outro)
- File Size: 40-60 MB
- Duration: ~90-120 minutes

**Metadata Chapters:**
```
Chapter 1: Prologue - 2:30
Chapter 2: Act 1, Scene 1 - Loft - 8:45
Chapter 3: Act 1, Scene 2 - Coffee Shop - 12:15
Chapter 4: Act 1, Scene 3 - Gathering - 5:30
Chapter 5: Act 2, Scene 1 - Groom's Room - 6:20
Chapter 6: Act 2, Scene 2 - Bride's Room - 5:45
Chapter 7: Act 2, Scene 3 - Ceremony Space Entrance - 8:10
Chapter 8: Act 2, Scene 4 - The Vows - 12:30
Chapter 9: Act 2, Scene 5 - Kiss & Celebration - 7:20
Chapter 10: Act 3, Scene 1 - Studio, Five Years Later - 6:45
Chapter 11: Act 3, Scene 2 - Rooftop - 7:15
Chapter 12: Epilogue - The Story Continues - 9:00
```

**Generation Process:**
```bash
# Concatenate audio files with proper ordering
ffmpeg -f concat -safe 0 -i audio_list.txt \
  -c copy CodeBase_Love_Story_Full.m4a

# Add metadata and chapters using mp4v2 or similar
mp4chaps --import CodeBase_chapters.txt \
  CodeBase_Love_Story_Full.m4a

# Create final M4B file
cp CodeBase_Love_Story_Full.m4a CodeBase_Love_Story_Audiobook.m4b
```

**Distribution Platforms:**
- Apple Books
- Audible/Amazon
- Google Play Books
- Spotify
- Other audiobook platforms
